SERVICE TRIPS

WHAT ARE MEDLIFE SERVICE TRIPS?

MEDLIFE Service Learning Trips allow for student participants to interact with underserved communities in South American countries by aiding in efforts relating to medicine, education and community development. Learning about social and health disparities firsthand on these trips is truly rewarding and reaffirms a student’s goal of working towards alleviating these disparities in their professional futures.

Each Service Learning Trip (SLT) lasts about 7-9 days depending on date of arrival and departure. Minimum number of registrants for each trip are listed on the main MEDLIFE webpage.

Locations for Spring Break International SLTs are announced within the first two UF MEDLIFE general body meetings, and trip information sessions and workshops are scheduled throughout the end of Fall semester and until the trip date.

HOW DO I REGISTER?

  • You must fill out a trip interest form to get added to our trip list! There you’ll receive updates on information sessions regarding registration, payment, itinerary and packing. You will also be notified of Spanish language, Cultural Sensitivity and vitals workshops to better prepare you for our trips.
  • All registration and payment are done online, so you must have access to a computer and internet.

WHAT DO WE DO ON SERVICE LEARNING TRIPS?

  • MEDLIFE’s main goal is to provide medical relief as well as education and development in low-income areas. Throughout the year, our UF chapter fundraises for our trips and equipment for use at the mobile clinics we set up. At the mobile clinic, participants have the chance to work closely with their local medical professionals, community leaders, and MEDLIFE staff. Students are given an assigned schedule upon arrival, and their rotations include shifts in General Physician shadowing, Dentistry, Pharmacy, OB/GYN (female volunteers only), Education, Triage, and Toothbrush Station. Clinic days are three out of the four days of work.
  • The remaining day of work is reserved for the community development project which is unique for each location. Infrastructure development projects is our other focus on these trips, to allow students to work alongside community members on a project that will aid their daily lives. Participants can learn about these communities beyond the medical perspective of the mobile clinics as they can interact with local citizens in their communities.

SCHOLARSHIPS

  • National MEDLIFE offers scholarships based on financial need and active member status. Once the trip dates get closer, applications for their scholarships are released and we will inform active members once they are open for submission.
  • Our own UF MEDLIFE chapter offers scholarships to active members that require a minimum point requirement along with a short essay prompt. To be an active member you must pay the membership fee and keep up with the point requirements every semester.

PREPARING FOR TRIPS

  • For international travel, you are required to have a passport with at least 6 months left prior to its expiration.
    • For example, if we have a trip in March and your passport expires in June you will not be allowed on the trip. You must renew your passport before any trip deadline, so double check to see if you’re good to go.
    • If we have a trip in February and your passport expires in November you’re good to go!
  • We will hold group information sessions regarding registering yourself for the trips and payments. In addition, we will hold Spanish language and cultural sensitivity workshops which will be required for all trip participants.
  • A tentative packing list is presented at the information sessions closer to the date of the trip, and if there are any required vaccines we will inform participants weeks in advance to allow enough time for immunizations. Check out this link to see what vaccines are required or recommended.
